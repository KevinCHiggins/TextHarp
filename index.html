TextHarp (prototype)

By Kevin Higgins, using Tone

Originally this was to be a blog post, but I used it as sample text for this audio experiment, and blogged about that instead!

Turn the volume up and try click on the text if you don't hear anything.

There are many, many amateur musicians in the world. Perhaps you are one of them. Hobbyist musicians often seem to confess to their status - "I'm not a real musician" is the sentiment. In various phrasings, that's something you can read again and again online in comment threads and forums.

A few months ago I myself retired from being a "real musician" and decided to find another full-time job. I quit completely because I was burnt out on music performance. Having been on both the pro and civilian sides, I feel like setting the record straight for all the amateurs. Mostly because I respect their love of music as much as anyone's and I feel sad when people are defensively self-deprecating. So if you dabble in music in any way, I want to ask four questions.

Do you play every day? Or even regularly? If so, I'm super impressed. Many who make a living from music don't practice regularly.

Do you like nice gear? I.e. do you perhaps resemble a certain well-known stereotype of the collector who knows all the model names but can't play too good? Well be proud of the nice stuff you own - lots of gigging musicians (young jazzers are particularly guilty of this) don't know their gear well, don't take care of it and play second-rate instruments.

Do you love music? Many performers don't, at least not all the time. I certainly didn't get much joy from it in the last few years. And there's the case of professionals who could get all the work they need, but dislike playing - preferring arranging or directing, say.

Finally, are you insecure about how good you are? If so, welcome to the club.

For most, playing music resembles nothing so much as hard work. Something I'd never disparage, whatever form it takes.
